Hello Max,

In twilight's hush where shadows grow,

Death walks softly, calm and slow.

No clang of chains, no wrathful cry—

Just silence draped across the sky.

She does not steal with cruel delight,

But folds the soul in velvet night.

A final kiss upon the brow,

A breath, a hush—we yield somehow.

Beneath the earth or stars above,

She guides with strangely tender love.

Not foe, not friend, but something more,

A keeper of the unseen door.

For every tear the living shed,

A dream is kindled for the dead.

And in that dream, the light returns—

A flame that even stillness burns.

So do not fear her sable wings,

Nor curse the hush her silence brings.

She is the pause, the sweet release,

The end of war, the start of peace.
